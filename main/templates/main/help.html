If theres a problem make sure your base is linearly independent
and that you follow the rules for valid inner product:
Positivity: ⟨v, v⟩ ≥ 0
For all vectors v in the vector space, the inner product ⟨v, v⟩ must be greater than or equal to zero.
Definiteness: ⟨v, v⟩=0 if and only if v=0
v is the zero vector.
Linearity in the First Argument: ⟨a⋅u + b⋅v, w⟩ = a⋅⟨u, w⟩ + b⋅⟨v, w⟩
for any scalars a and b and vectors u, v.
Conjugate Symmetry: ⟨u, v⟩ = ⟨v, u⟩
For complex vector spaces, the inner product must be conjugate symmetric.
